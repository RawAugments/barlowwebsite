About


The Barlow family, which was only started in 2017 is a typeface with many different options for users to choose from. The lead of this project is named Jeremy Tribby. This “Grotesque” font got it’s name after EFF co-founder, activist, songwriter, and cattle rancher John Perry Barlow. They named the font after him as a tribute for his contribution with the Information Superhighway. The Information Superhighway was a popular term used through the 1990’s to refer to digital communication systems and the Internet telecommunications network, just in case you weren’t sure what that was like myself. Barlow can be described as a “slightly rounded, low-contrast, grotesque type family”.


Tribby is based outta San Francisco, California where he makes fonts all day, every day for big companies. He has been in the typeface business for over 21 years overall. In the past he worked at the Electronic Frontier Foundation(EFF), and nowadays works as a design technologist at Plaid. Tribby has made another font called “Galiano” and continues to work on more to this day. Digging more into the nature andstyle of the font, Grotesque was the first form of sans serif type. It appeared in the early 19th Century, and had a somewhat irregular and awkward form (hence the name). The more refined evolution of this style, popular in the mid 20th century, became known as Neo-grotesque. Compared to say Gothic style, there is no difference between the 2 styles since their terms used during the nineteenth century.
